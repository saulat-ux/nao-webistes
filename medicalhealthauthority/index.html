Frequently Asked Questions

You should see a doctor if you experience persistent symptoms that interfere with daily activities, symptoms that worsen over time, or any concerning changes in your health. For emergency symptoms like chest pain, difficulty breathing, severe headache, or signs of stroke, seek immediate medical attention.
Adults should have a comprehensive physical exam annually, though frequency may vary based on age, health status, and risk factors. Your healthcare provider can recommend the appropriate schedule for your individual health needs and screening requirements.
Adults typically need annual flu vaccines, COVID-19 boosters as recommended, and periodic vaccines like Tdap (tetanus, diphtheria, pertussis). Additional vaccines may be recommended based on age, health conditions, travel plans, and occupation. Consult your healthcare provider for personalized recommendations.
Prepare by listing your symptoms, current medications, questions you want to ask, and bringing your insurance card and ID. Consider bringing a family member for support, arrive early, and be ready to discuss your medical history and any concerns you may have.
Urgent care is for non-life-threatening conditions that need prompt attention, like minor injuries, flu symptoms, or infections. Emergency rooms are for serious, life-threatening conditions like chest pain, severe injuries, difficulty breathing, or stroke symptoms. Emergency rooms are typically more expensive and have longer wait times.
